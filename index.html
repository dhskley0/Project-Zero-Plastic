🌿 Plastic Pollution Awareness

Every year, the world produces over 380 million tons of plastic — and much of it ends up in oceans, rivers, and landfills. Plastic pollution affects marine life, human health, and contributes to climate change. The goal of Project Zero Plastic is to inspire people to reduce plastic use and support sustainable alternatives.


🌱 Together, we can start with small steps — saying no to single-use plastics, bringing reusable bags, and spreading awareness in our communities.

🌊 What is Plastic Pollution?

Plastic pollution occurs when plastic materials accumulate in the environment, harming ecosystems, animals, and humans. Most plastics are non-biodegradable, lasting hundreds of years and breaking down into microplastics that infiltrate water, soil, and air.

♻️ Solutions and Daily Actions

  • Reduce: Avoid single-use plastics whenever possible.
  • Reuse: Bring your own bottles, containers, and eco-bags.
  • Recycle: Properly sort and dispose of waste.
  • Support: Eco-friendly businesses and join cleanup drives.

Awareness is the first step — action makes change.

📊 Plastic Waste Statistics

  • Worldwide: Over 400 million tons of plastic waste are produced each year, and only 9% is recycled.
  • Philippines: Around 2.7 million tons of plastic waste are generated annually, with 20% ending up in the ocean.
  • Provinces & Cities: Metro Manila contributes about 25% of total plastic waste, followed by Cebu and Davao.
  • Barangays: Each barangay produces 1–3 tons of plastic waste monthly, mostly from single-use plastics.

⚖️ Environmental Laws & Efforts

  • RA 9003: Ecological Solid Waste Management Act of 2000 — focuses on waste segregation and recycling.
  • RA 8749: Clean Air Act — reduces pollution from burning waste materials.
  • RA 9275: Clean Water Act — protects water bodies from pollution caused by improper waste disposal.
  • RA 11898: Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) Act — requires companies to recover and recycle plastic packaging.

What is Plastic Pollution?

Plastic pollution occurs when plastic materials accumulate in the environment, harming ecosystems, animals, and humans. Most plastics are non-biodegradable, lasting hundreds of years, breaking down into microplastics that infiltrate water, soil, and air.

Common sources include single-use items like bags, bottles, wrappers, and straws. These often end up in rivers and oceans, threatening marine life and natural habitats.

Why It Matters

Plastic pollution affects every corner of the planet, from the deepest oceans to the air we breathe. Animals mistake plastics for food or get entangled, leading to injury or death. Microplastics have even entered our food chain, posing risks to human health.

The impact extends to tourism, fisheries, and local economies. Reducing plastic use protects both nature and people, ensuring a cleaner, healthier future.
